Always use buttons on forms
5 October 2016

Modern websites tend to take the approach of doing everything for the user. Said approach seems harmless, correct? Not exactly.

Take for instance updating your profile settings.

You type all of your information into the fields. Your name, a biography, your location, et cetera. Then you go to save it and..

Wait, that's already been done for you.

So why do people do it?

You see, after using websites for any period of time, you understand the flow of doing something like this. You navigate to the Update profile link, fill the proper information, and press "save."

When saving is done automatically, it interrupts this flow.

Even though, in theory, it saves the user time. He or she may be confused by what just happened. Of course, some sort of message is displayed (or should be displayed) to the user, regardless.

Saving... Your profile settings have been updated.
