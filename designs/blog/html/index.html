About Me

Hello there. My name is Zak Nesler. I am a sixteen year old who loves to write code. I had started to do this when I was in middle school; ever since then, I have been addicted to it. It all started out with some basic HTML and CSS. From that point, I started to learn Batch, Lua following suit. After a while of playing around with those languages, I took an interest in back-end web development.

I roughly taught myself PHP at the beginning, after which I discovered Codecademy. I learned more about PHP there, as well as improving my knowledge with HTML and CSS. In addition to that, I also learned some Ruby and Python. PHP was definitely the language that stuck with me, though.

Shortly after that, I happened upon Codecourse (formerly phpacademy). If you may permit to me a plug: the creator of Codecourse, Alex Garrett, provides very professional and extremely easy-to-follow tutorials. I learned a lot more about PHP, and just about every language pertinent to web-development, just by watching and following along with his videos. In addition to that, he also led me on to Laravel.

Laravel—a PHP framework—always was a confusing and daunting thing that I tried my absolute hardest to avoid. Every time I saw the word "Laravel," I shivered because I was so scared (exaggerating). It took me the longest time to get myself to watch something Laravel-esque. When I did, however, I realized that it was possibly the greatest thing since—bad joke incoming—sliced bread.

In addition to learning Laravel from Codecourse, and of course reading its documentation, I learned a large amount from Laracasts. It has literal tons (okay, maybe not literal) of tutorials that were created by Jeffery Way. With these three resources, I mastered Laravel in only a few months. If you are interested in Laravel, I recommend that you do, too.
